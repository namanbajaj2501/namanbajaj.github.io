Resources

A curated collection of resources for astronomy internships and Ph.D. opportunities. Too often, opportunities are missed simply because they’re not widely visible. This page aims to make them easier to find. If you know of a great resource that must be included here, please send it my way using the Contact page.

Research Internships/ Schools/ Workshops

A few good starting points for finding internships, summer schools, and workshops in astronomy/planet formation.

  • Internships in India by Shravya — A list of internship opportunities for Indian students in India and abroad. It also contains a small set of opportunities, especially for Indian women.
  • Useful online material on Astronomy by Bhavesh — A unique list that contains links to Online courses, Python tutorials, Grad school guides, DIY website building for academia, and more.
  • Pathways to science — An unending list of research internships and other space-related opportunities in the US. Many require US citizenship to be eligible.
  • Harvard list of internships — A small but useful list of astronomy-related research internships in the US and abroad.
  • Projectyl physics internships in India — A list of internships and summer/winter schools/workshops across the world with a special focus on India.
  • Astrophysics summer programs by Stephen Turban — A small yet impactful list of astrophysics programs for high-school students mainly in the US.
  • Black in Astro — A list of the most famous astronomy research internship programs across the world, open to various nationalities.
  • Zapplyjob list of internships — A relatively generic list of science research internships across the world. Some postings are specific to Indian students.

Ph.D. Positions and Admissions

Links to pages that aggregate Ph.D. openings and general admissions guidance/timelines.

  • The complete guide by astrophysicsgirl — A unique list of funded Masters program in astronomy + research internships and workshops across the world + guide on how to apply for Ph.D.
  • The ultimate resource by Floor Broekgaarden — I should have checked this while applying for Ph.D. myself -- the ultimate guide to Ph.D. and Postdoc applications, from programs list to application preparation to interview tips.
  • All you need info on US and Canada Ph.D. applications — GRE requirements and admissions fees for US/Canadian Astronomy & Physics Programs
  • Storyteller Scientist — Curated list of mostly Ph.D. and Postdoc positions, but also contains internship opportunities.
  • Resources by Skylar Grayson — An amazing list of guides on how to apply for Grad School (Ph.D.), Postdoc, and fellowship/funding.
  • The Astrobetter Rumor Mill — A community-driven list of Postdoc and term positions (and fellowships) that is updated very regularly and used widely.
  • AAS Division for Planetary Sciences — An almost exhaustive list of graduate programs (MS and Ph.D.) in planetary sciences in the US, Europe, and the UK
  • AAS Division for Solar Physics — An almost exhaustive list of graduate programs (MS and Ph.D.) in solar physics in the US and other countries
  • American Institute of Physics — The ultimate list of all the US universities that provide Bachelors, Masters, or Ph.D. in Physics and/or Astronomy with critical program stats.
  • Astrobetter article by Yvette Cendes — A quick starter on how to decide between Master's and Ph.D. in Europe, and which are some of the most famous programs. The comment section has some useful discussions too.
  • Indigo consortium (Gwaves) — An (incomplete) list of research institutes offering PhD programs in Gravitational-Wave Astronomy and related fields in North America, Europe, Asia, Australia (and India, separately).
  • Indian astronomy institutes by Asmita Bhandare — A near-complete list of Indian institutes offering Master's and Ph.D. programs (including integrated ones) in Astronomy.
  • How to write your SOP — Not a list but really the ultimate guide to writing your Statement of Purpose (SOP).
