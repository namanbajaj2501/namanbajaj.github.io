Research Interests

Planet Formation in Protoplanetary Disks

My primary research goal is to understand how planets form within gaseous and dusty disks surrounding young stars. These disks set the initial conditions for planet formation and strongly influence the final properties of planetary systems.

Disk Evolution and Gas Dispersal

I study how gas is removed from disks through processes such as disk winds and photoevaporation. Gas dispersal determines the time available for giant planet formation and affects the migration and composition of forming planets.

Methods

  • Multi-wavelength observations of protoplanetary disks
  • Theoretical modeling of disk physics
  • Numerical simulations of disk winds and evolution
