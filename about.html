About Me

I am an astronomer working on the formation and evolution of planetary systems. My research focuses on the physical processes operating in protoplanetary disks, particularly during the late stages when gas is dispersed and planetary architectures are finalized.

I use a combination of observational data, theoretical modeling, and numerical simulations to study disk winds, gas chemistry, and the interaction between forming planets and their natal environments.

My broader interests include disk evolution, star–planet interactions, and the connection between disk properties and exoplanet populations.
